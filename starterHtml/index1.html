What makes Canada an attractive destination to Indians?

By Varun Singh

Canada has emerged as the most popular destination for Indians who want to migrate abroad for various reasons, such as education, work, business, or investment. According to the latest data from the Ministry of External Affairs, nearly 1.6 lakh Indians, accounting for 20% of those renouncing their citizenship, have opted for Canadian nationality between January 2018 and June 2023.

Canada has become the second most preferred destination for Indian expatriates after the United States, which saw 2.4 lakh Indians acquiring its citizenship in the same period.

Canada Immigration Policies

Canada has a point-based system that assesses applicants based on their skills, education, language proficiency, work experience, and other criteria. This system is transparent, fair, and flexible, allowing qualified candidates to apply for permanent residency or citizenship through various programs, such as the Express Entry, the Provincial Nominee Program, the Atlantic Immigration Pilot, and the Global Talent Stream. Canada also offers a fast-track process for skilled workers and entrepreneurs who can contribute to its economy and innovation.

High quality of life: Canada is ranked among the top countries in the world for its quality of life, according to the UN Human Development Index. Canada offers a safe, stable, and multicultural society, with a high standard of living, health care, education, and social security. Canada also has a diverse and beautiful natural environment, with four distinct seasons and plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Excellent education opportunities: Canada is home to some of the best universities and colleges in the world, offering a wide range of programs and degrees for international students. Canada also has a reputation for academic excellence, research, and innovation, attracting many students who want to pursue higher education and career development. Canada also provides generous scholarships, grants, and loans for eligible students, as well as work permits and post-graduation work opportunities for graduates.
